Our Story — From Cold War to Forever ❤️
It all began in 2019, when we were in Class 9. Until then, our school had no girls—only in Class 9...
It all began in 2019, when we were in Class 9. Until then, our school had no girls—only in Class 9 did girls start joining through transfers. That year, everything felt different, and that’s when she entered my life, though neither of us knew what was about to begin. On her very first day, she noticed me talking to another girl and immediately judged me, calling me “দালাল” in her mind. Later, when she saw that I didn’t really talk to any girls in class, she changed my title to “Mr. Attitude.” And me? Seeing how strict, disciplined, and commanding she was, always leading the girls and taking control of situations, I secretly gave her a nickname—“Miss Hitler.” It wasn’t out of hate, but because of the strong aura she carried that made everyone listen to her. As more girls joined the class, a strange kind of cold war started between boys and girls. If one boy made a mistake, the entire group of boys would be blamed. And leading the girls’ side was her—confident, bold, and always ready to stand up. I never really got involved in those fights; I stayed away from all the drama. But one day, the boys had a meeting to figure out how to handle the situation, and someone suggested that if their leader could be won over, everything would calm down. Somehow, they chose me for the task, probably because I had just gone through a breakup and was single. Without thinking much, I agreed and said I would make her fall for me. At that time, it was just a plan—nothing more. On 3rd September 2019, I made my first move by sending her a message through her friend, telling her that I liked her. She wasn’t the kind of girl who believed in relationships. She was a topper, focused on studies, and had already rejected many boys without hesitation. So honestly, I didn’t have much hope. But instead of rejecting me, she said something unexpected—she told me to wait. I asked her how long, jokingly saying one week, one month, or two months. She looked at me and said, “One year.” She thought I would give up, that this was just a test I wouldn’t pass. But I calmly said yes, that I would wait. From that day, I didn’t try to force anything. Instead, I slowly became a part of her daily life. I would sit on her seat and wait for her. When she arrived, she would tell me to go back to my place. During class, I would quietly look at her, and she would turn back and tell me to focus on studies instead of her. But somewhere in between, she started liking it. Without realizing, she got used to my presence. I had become a part of her routine. Then one day, I didn’t go to school. That day, something changed. Later, I came to know that she wasn’t herself—she didn’t ask anyone directly, but her eyes kept searching for me. She felt my absence, and that’s when I understood that she had started missing me. That’s when I decided it was time for the final step. On 23rd September 2019, I went to school and completely ignored her. I didn’t look at her, didn’t talk to her, didn’t react at all. In class, even when she turned back to look at me, I avoided eye contact. She felt the sudden change, the silence, the absence of everything she had slowly gotten used to. After a few classes, I went up to her desk, placed my hand firmly on it, stood right in front of her, and told her to say whatever she wanted to say that day itself. She got nervous. Her friends were already pushing her, telling her to say yes or she would regret it. Still, she couldn’t say anything at that moment. Later, when I was going to submit my scholarship documents, she and her friend stopped me on the way. She wanted to say something, but instead started talking about some issue in my documents. I took the papers from her hand and walked away, still maintaining that serious, distant attitude. I continued ignoring her the entire day. After the last class, she was waiting outside. Only a few of us were left in the classroom when she called me out. I already knew what she was going to say, but I kept a calm and serious expression. Then, in a single moment, she said, “Don’t tell anyone, but from my side, it’s a yes.” For a second, I was completely shocked. I just stood there, not moving, not reacting. My plan had worked—but in that moment, it didn’t feel like a plan anymore. What started as a simple challenge, almost like a game, slowly turned into something real. With time, we both fell in love—truly, deeply, and loyally. It was no longer about winning or proving anything. It became about understanding, caring, and being there for each other. And that’s how I became the reason a girl who once didn’t believe in love or relationships… finally started believing in it. ❤️

Our First Everything: From Ayodhya Hills to Raas Mela ✨🚌
​The day began in the familiar surroundings of the Chelyama Computer Training Center, but December 24, 2023, was destined to be a massive milestone in our story—our...
The day began in the familiar surroundings of the Chelyama Computer Training Center, but December 24, 2023, was destined to be a massive milestone in our story—our very first trip and first picnic together! 🎒🌿 The center had organized a highly anticipated getaway to the breathtaking Ayodhya Hills, and the excitement among all of us was palpable. Boarding the bus with our classmates, the air was thick with laughter, music, and endless chatter 🎶. Yet, amidst the vibrant noise, we found our own quiet sanctuary simply by sitting next to each other. The long drive slipped by effortlessly; every passing mile felt entirely special just because Manu (Mandira) was by my side ❤️. After hours on the road, we finally arrived at the Lower Dam of Ayodhya Hills. The view was breathtaking—serene waters framed by green hills and touched by a gentle, refreshing breeze 🍃. Almost immediately, the teachers began assigning tasks, directing most of the girls to prep for the cooking. But in that moment, I couldn’t bear the thought of losing her to the busy crowd. Quietly, I pulled her away, and we walked down toward the dam together 🌊. Away from the responsibilities and the noise, time seemed to slow down just for us. The calm surroundings and the comfortable silence between us felt like magic ✨. In that quiet corner, we captured a simple couple photo 📸. It wasn’t just a picture; it was a memory stolen from time itself, a quiet testament to our bond that we would carry forever. ​As the afternoon faded into evening, we boarded the bus for the journey back 🚌🌅. The atmosphere had shifted from energetic excitement to a comfortable, contented exhaustion. The bus was filled with tired faces, soft laughter, and quiet, reflective moments. Somewhere between the lingering noise of our friends and the peaceful silence between us, we snapped another photo—a simple, unplanned memory that perfectly captured the quiet joy of the ride home 🌙. But the night had one last beautiful surprise in store for us: our first mela! 🎡 As darkness settled, our bus made a stop in Purulia so we could experience the vibrant Raas Mela. Stepping out, we were instantly enveloped by a world of glowing, colorful lights, bustling crowds, rhythmic music, and the rich aroma of street food 🍡✨. The contrast from the quiet hills was exhilarating. As soon as we entered the mela, Manu (Mandira) grabbed my hand tightly 🤝. With eyes full of excitement, she pulled me through the dense, bustling crowd. It felt as though she was leading me into her own little universe of carefree happiness 🌌. We wandered past the vibrant stalls, smiling, laughing, and entirely absorbed in each other's company 🥰. Amidst the lively chaos, we bought two matching flower pots 🪴 to commemorate the night. Then, in a quiet gesture, I picked out a simple but beautiful hair clip for her 🎀—a tiny gift carrying a thousand unspoken feelings. Under the glow of the mela lights, surrounded by the noise of the world but completely lost in our own, our first trip, first picnic, and first mela became a perfect, unforgettable chapter in our story 📖💖.

Our First Tea Date ☕
The morning of May 23rd, 2025, began like any other, but it held the promise of something entirely new. The clock had just struck 7:00 AM, and the town was still slowly waking up...
The morning of May 23rd, 2025, began like any other, but it held the promise of something entirely new. The clock had just struck 7:00 AM, and the town was still slowly waking up. A gentle morning breeze swept through the Raghunathpur bus stand as we found a quiet corner in the waiting room. Before the heavy books and the daily rush of our tuition classes could consume us, this stolen pocket of time belonged entirely to us. We ordered tea, and the vendor handed it to us in traditional matir cups (earthen clay cups). I can still remember the rich, sweet aroma of the boiling tea mingling with the rustic, baked-earth scent of the clay. Holding the warm bhaar in our hands to chase away the slight morning chill, we sat close together. We didn’t even need grand conversations; the simple, quiet comfort of sharing that steaming tea in an empty waiting room was magic. It was our very first tea date, tucked away beautifully in the most ordinary of places. Eventually, the clock reminded us of our responsibilities, and we had to part ways for our different tuition batches, stepping out of our peaceful bubble and into the busy morning. But the lingering warmth of the tea and the anticipation of seeing each other again made the long study hours fly by. Days later, unable to resist reliving that simple happiness, we found ourselves back at the same bus stand waiting room for another quiet tea date. It felt just as special, as if time had paused again only for us. After finishing our tea, I started my bike and dropped her off at her tuition. It wasn’t anything grand, just a short ride through familiar roads, but with her sitting behind me, holding on, it felt like the journey itself had become a memory worth keeping forever.

Our First Temple Visit as a Couple
​It was the 10th of June, 2025, just before my birthday, when our paths converged at the peaceful grounds of the Moutorh Mata Kali Mandir...
It was the 10th of June, 2025, just before my birthday, when our paths converged at the peaceful grounds of the Moutorh Mata Kali Mandir. We hadn't traveled together; she journeyed from her home, and I from mine. But the moment we finally met at the temple steps, it felt as though our individual journeys had always been leading us right to each other. Before entering, we stopped to gather offerings, sharing a quiet, unspoken gesture of love. Instead of buying prasad for ourselves, I bought the sacred sweets for her, and she bought them for me—a tender exchange of care before we even approached the deity. As we crossed the threshold into the mandir, our hands found one another. We walked forward, reaching up to ring every single temple bell together—har ek ghanti ko sath main bajate huye. The chimes echoed through the sacred halls in perfect harmony, a beautiful melody carrying the sound of our shared devotion. Side by side, we knelt before the divine presence of Mata Kali. With bowed heads and full hearts, we sought her ashirwad, silently asking for her grace to guide us. That serene day was so much more than just a quiet prayer; it was our very first temple visit as a couple. It was a perfectly blessed beginning to the beautiful journey unfolding before us.

Under the Saptami Sky: Our First Mela Adventure
On September 29, 2025, the festive day of Durga Puja Saptami, we went on our very first mela adventure with our school friends. To keep it a secret from her family, I waited and sneaked into the car only after...
The afternoon of September 29, 2025, wasn't just any ordinary day—it was the vibrant day of Durga Puja Saptami, and it marked the beginning of our very first mela experience together. The whole plan started with a thrilling little secret. To make sure her family wouldn't find out, we had to be clever. When our common school friends brought the car around 2 PM, she got in first. I waited patiently down the road, and only after the car had safely passed her house did I jump in. The moment I sat beside her, the adventure truly began.

Our first stop was Bhamoria. The mela was alive with festive energy, but in that crowded place, my world was just her. We spent the entire afternoon exploring the grounds, our fingers laced tightly together as we walked hand in hand through the bustling stalls. We stopped to take so many pictures, freezing those bright smiles and perfect moments in time. We shared spicy bhelpuri and sipped on cold drinks, enjoying the simple magic of being together. I bought her a beautiful pair of jhumkas that I knew would look stunning on her, and showing her incredibly pure heart, she bought a sweet gift for my mother.

As the evening approached, we made our way to Nituria. The mela there had its own charm, and we continued our stroll, still refusing to let go of each other's hands. Suddenly, a light shower of rain began to fall. Instead of letting it ruin the fun, it gave us one of the most romantic moments of the day. We quickly ducked under the extended awning of a small shop to take shelter. Standing close together under that tin roof, watching the rain fall around us, was a quiet, intimate memory I will never forget.

Once the rain cleared, our journey took us to Raghunathpur, where we stopped at Sudamrit to have dinner. Since it was Puja and she was strictly following a vegetarian diet that day, her options were limited. She ordered sweet fried rice and karai paneer. I know her so well—I knew exactly how much she usually eats and that she would never be able to finish a full plate by herself. So, I purposely only ordered a single egg roll for myself. Just as I expected, she couldn't finish her meal, and I happily ate her jhutha (leftovers). Sharing food like that just made me feel even closer to her.

But as the night wore on and it was time to head back, the joyful energy slowly turned into a heavy, bittersweet quiet. The ride from Bhamoria back towards home was deeply emotional. She rested her head gently on my shoulder for the entire journey. I could feel how sad she was that the day was ending. The thought of going back to our separate homes, of being apart after such a perfect day, weighed heavily on both of us. It was a comfortable silence, but one filled with the ache of not wanting to say goodbye.

Around 7 or 8 PM, we finally reached Moutorh. I dropped her off, and we shared a reluctant, heartfelt goodbye. As I watched her go, my heart was completely full. That day was a beautiful whirlwind of secrets, rain, shared food, and endless love—our very first mela, and a memory I will treasure forever.

Our First Rasoi
The temple near my house is built by my family, and the priest there is my middle brother. One day, he told her to keep 16 Somvar fast...
The temple near my house is built by my family, and the priest there is my middle brother. One day, he told her to keep 16 Somvar fast and said, "If you complete it with faith, your wish will come true, and after that you should make kheer and offer it to God." I already knew what her wish was--she wanted to marry me. She started her vrat on 8th September and completed it on 22 December 2025, but that day she couldn't come to the temple, so everything felt a little incomplete. Then on 29 December, the 17th Monday, she finally came. What made that day even more special was my family--everyone already liked her as my future wife, and they treated her with so much warmth and happiness. No one said anything directly, but their behavior showed everything. The plan was always to offer kheer, but what happened next was something I never expected. Instead of making it in the temple, my brother brought her into our home kitchen to cook. That moment felt different… special. It felt like she already belonged to this house. I was sent to bring milk, and even that small thing felt important to me. She was cooking kheer, and I was just standing there watching her--no words, no drama, just a calm and real moment. After that, she offered the kheer to God in the temple and distributed it as prasad to everyone. Nothing was official that day, but for me, that was our first rasoi--unexpected, simple, and one of the most beautiful memories of my life. 💖
